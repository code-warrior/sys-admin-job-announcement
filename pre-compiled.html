CS Dept Seeks Student System Administrators

The Dept of Computing Sciences seeks candidates to join our team of system administrators, or sys admins, currently consisting of four students and a faculty supervisor. Sys admins maintain hardware and software systems used by over 100 faculty, staff, and students — on and off campus. Two positions are available for immediate occupancy.

qualifications

Candidates should possess at least the following required qualifications, with preference given to those with preferred experience.

Required

  • cs, hcid, or cse major
  • First-year or sophomore standing, as of spring 2023
  • Intend to graduate from uhart
  • Good analytical writing skills
  • Laser-focused task commitment in order to meet given deadlines
  • Neat, organized, and detail-oriented
  • Ability to work extended and irregular hours in the rare case that the need arises
  • Some level of passion for technology
  • A curious nature to solve problems, even when not possessing all the knowledge required to do so
  • Effective team collaboration skills

Preferred

  • Some experience working inside computers, such as upgrading ram, exchanging power supply units, or switching out storage devices
  • Any experience installing and/or configuring servers
  • Some skill with Linux/unix
  • Involvement with network management, either in Windows, Linux, or both
  • Some experience in writing bash scripts
  • Skill in working with Markdown syntax
  • Experience filing issues in Trello, ZenHub, JIRA, or other kanban-like systems
  • Project experience working with Git, including branching, forking, cloning, and pull requests
  • Have prepared and/or maintained CAT 5 network cables

Responsibilities

  • Practice semi-agile methodologies via weekly scrum meetings
  • Create and manage user accounts
  • Install or upgrade hardware in classrooms, server rooms, and faculty/staff spaces
  • Troubleshoot hardware/software issues
  • Implement new features to our network and/or computer systems
  • Write clear and concise issues in order to effectively document problems with our systems
  • Aide in the hiring process of other sys admins
  • Physically install or replace computers, keyboards, monitors, projects, and/or network infrastructure

Upon graduation, you will have gained real-world, hands-on experience working on live systems in a team atmosphere.

Note: This role has the potential to be very physical. For example, heavy, 30-lb servers need to be shuffled around inside a 7-ft server rack. From time to time, you will have to replace classroom computers by crawling into awkward physical situations, such as crouching under a desk in a classroom.

Benefits

  • $14 per hour, with an average work week of 10 hours per week1
  • Flexible work hours
  • Real-world technical experience
  • Develop team-building skills
  • Preparation to work in a dev ops-related field
